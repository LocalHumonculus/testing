According to discoverymood.com, self-harm is the act of emotionally or physically harming oneself. This is normally executed via cutting with a blade, burning skin, picking at skin, pulling hair, etc.

What is SH?

People that self-harm typically have undergone intense traumas or stressful circumstances which cause them to need a way to outlet pent up emotions. Self-harm is a coping mechanism used to replace a feeling of emptiness with pain and/or release strong emotions in a way that acts as a “relief” to a person. https://medlineplus.gov/selfharm.html

Why do people SH?

One of the best things you can do if you know someone who regularly self-harms is to not tell an authority figure. This may heighten the stress levels of the person and worsen their self-destructive behavior. Most importantly, let them know that you are there for them no matter what. Always be there for them, they need it. In addition to this, never make someone seem like their self-harming is a part of them, because self-harm is a behavior, not a personality trait. Support them for who THEY are as a person, not solely because they’re struggling. mind.org.uk

How do you comfort someone who SH?

Considering the fact that self-harm is mostly a pain-based coping mechanism, one of the best ways to fight this urge is wearing a hair tie or rubber band that simulates pain when you snap it against your skin, yet it does no harm. Along with using a rubber band, using something like an ice pack on your chest to focus your thoughts on the cold sensation is something that will also slow your heart rate down. Despite these methods, they’re only temporary relief and the best way to deal with thoughts of harming yourself is talking to a professional therapist that can provide you with advice and other ways to resist your intrusive thoughts. relevancerecovery.com
